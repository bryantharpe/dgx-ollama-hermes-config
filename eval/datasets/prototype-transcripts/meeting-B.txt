[Standup retrospective, two speakers — A is leading, B is engineer]

A: So we keep saying we're going to track standup updates somewhere and we
keep not doing it. Can we just build a small dashboard for it? Nothing fancy.

B: Sure, what does it need to do.

A: Each person on the team posts a quick update — what they worked on
yesterday, what they're working on today, anything blocking them. Three
fields. And it gets timestamped. Then we want to be able to look back at the
history.

B: How many people on the team?

A: Right now it's five — me, you, Sam, Priya, Marcus. Just hardcode them as
options in a dropdown for now, we can make it more flexible later.

A: What I really want is two views. A "today" view that shows everyone's
update from today, with empty slots for people who haven't posted. So I can
see at a glance who hasn't checked in. And then a "history" view where I can
filter by person — like show me everything Marcus has said for the last two
weeks.

B: Got it. Filter by date range too?

A: Yeah, definitely. A from-date / to-date pair on the history view.

B: What about a chart or something visual? You said dashboard.

A: One simple chart would be nice — number of updates per day across the
whole team, just to see if participation is dropping off. A bar chart, last
30 days, nothing fancy. The chart can be on the today page, like at the top.

B: SQLite for storage?

A: Yeah, all local. I want this running on the box. The "today" view should
auto-refresh, like every minute or so, so I don't have to keep reloading.

B: Edit/delete on past updates?

A: Just delete, no edit. If you mistype, delete and re-post. Keeps the
schema simpler.

A: Slug — let's call it standup-tracker. And seed it with a couple of
example updates from yesterday and today, so when it starts up it's not
totally blank.
